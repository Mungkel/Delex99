How To Build The Delex99


Required parts:

- Microcontroller with more 25 digital pins or more, I used a teensy 4.1

- either a large 3d printer or access to a 3d printing service like PCBway (not sponsored, there other services) or a CNC if you're really fancy and want to make it from metal

- a soldering iron and cable (best if multiple different colors)

- 101 MX-style switches and keycaps fitting to your desired design

- a cable fitting to your Microcontroller or adapter

- 500mm foam or rubber gaskets that are 2-3mm wide

- 62 6x2 round magnets for the case (31 if the topcase is made from a magnetic material)

(optional) dampening material for the case

- diodes for every row

- dampening material to your desire (optional)


Building the keyboard:


1. Get the parts STL available on MakerWorld (for every download I get a certain amout of MakerWorld Coins to buy filament and 3d printers to make these projects work)

2. Inserting the switches into the plate, align the switches to the plate a little force may be required, but then an audible snap should be hearable as the tooth from the switch grip unto the plate.

3. Glue the magnets into the mounting holes. The mounting holes in the bottom case are marked with being holes and the topcase has slight indentations for the magnets.

4. The gaskets need to be glued to bottomcase. In the bottomcase you can add your optional dampening material

5. Wire the Keyboard according to the wiring diagram under this step.

6. download arduion IDE and the necessary packages for your microcontroller. Open the firmware file and upload it to the microcontroller

6.1. customise the keymap by working in the keymap file

7. place the plates inside the case and attach the topcase

enjoy your board
